SPECIAL APPRECIATION

I owe my computer literacy to a number of people. I remember getting the first taste of computer programming when I was 18. A college classmate taught me how to print "Hello World" using C-Free. I got hooked straight away and decided to venture into the computer world. I was terrible and beyond incompetent back then, but this classmate of mine remained super tolerant with my struggles and dedicated hours upon hours each week to assist me in my programming journey. Fast forward two years later, I am now able to code in multiple languages such as C, Java, and MySQL.

I didn't include html and css in the list becuase they are not "officially" classed as programming languages. But learning web-development became exceedingly easy with my prior exposure to more 'traditional' programming. And it is all thanks to this classmate of mine who were so dedicated in mentoring me. I genuinely cannot thank him enough.

Then comes the Final-Year-Project season. I decided that this mentor of mine deserved a break from my nonsense (jk haha) so I took a different route with my self-learning. The reality of not relying on my mentor anymore is scary beyond words. Luckily the universe heard my plea for help and here I am today with THC. Very great community with a lot of inspiring people. Some were decently experienced, others were complete beginners. It's fun to have found someone on "my level" of learning.
